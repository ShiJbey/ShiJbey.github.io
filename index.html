I graduated from Carnegie Mellon University with my M.S. in Biomedical Engineering and graduated from the University of Delaware with my B.S., double majoring in Computer Science and Neuroscience.

During my time in graduate school, I conducted research on electroencephalography (EEG)-based brain-computer interfaces for virtual reality.

Following grad school, I transitioned into the area of artificial intelligence/machine learning. My core interest has always been game development and over my matriculation at CMU I gained an additional interest in multi-agent machine learning. I firmly believe these technologies have great potential for creating immersive gameplay experiences.
