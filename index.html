I am a graduate student in the Department of Biomedical Engineering at Carnegie Mellon University. I earned my Bachelor of Science degree from the University of Delaware with a double major in Computer Science and Neuroscience. My current research focus is on the use of electroencephalography (EEG) brain-computer interfaces (BCI) in conjuction with virtual reality/mixed reality platforms. My personal interests revolve around game development and art. Below you will find descriptions of current and past projects. Enjoy.

Current Research

Audio-ERP for Virtual Interactions

This is my current project for my masters thesis. The goal is to develop a system by which a user, wearing an EEG headset, may be able to select objects in virtual space by focusing on a unique sound emitted from an object of interest.

Current Side Projects

An introduction to Machine Learning with Boxing Agents

I threw this project together over the course of a week as an entry in Unity's first challenge for their new 'Ml-Agents' machine learning workflow. I had tossed around the idea of using machine learning to train AI agents in fighing games, but had never gotten around to it until then. The agents learn via Reinforcement Learning and are given a simple policy that positively rewards landed punches on the opponent, and negatively awards taking damage to oneself. Other behaviors such as successful blocking, and advancing on the opponent are rewarded as well.

Github Link: Reinforcement Learning Boxing Bots

Infiniforge: My introduction to Computer Graphics And Procedural Generation

Inspired by the then upcoming release of 'No Man's Sky' and my interest in blacksmithing, I tried my hand at developing a PCG system for swords. My goal was to finish the project and submit it to the Reddit PCG challenge linked below. This project served as an introduction to both PCG as well as computer graphics. The result is a NodeJs server that can export 3D meshes to Unity via a REST API. A non-server web version is available to play with on this site (see 'Infiniforge' tab above).
