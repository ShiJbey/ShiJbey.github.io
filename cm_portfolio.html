Interpretive Projects

Below are links to the research publications that resulted from my work at Johns Hopkins University. At the time, I was an undergraduate research intern in Dr.Niebur's Computational Neuroscience lab. I worked on a project team with a Ph.D. student and another undergraduate. The goal of the project was to investigate what role head movements play in the visual exploration of images in VR. My job was to develop a VR environment that could present images to participants and track/record their head movements. After my job was complete, another undergraduate student was responsible for conducting live-trials.

  • Head movements during visual exploration of natural images in virtual reality

  • Head movements are correlated with other measures of visual attention at smaller spatial scales
